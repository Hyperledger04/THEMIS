# GOLD-STANDARD TEMPLATE: Application for Temporary Injunction under Order XXXIX Rules 1 & 2, CPC

## Format Rules
- Numbered paragraphs throughout (this is a court pleading, not a letter).
- Bold the prayer.
- Include verification by the deponent.
- Attach as application in suit or file as standalone IA.
- The three tests: (1) prima facie case, (2) balance of convenience, (3) irreparable harm/injury.

---

APPLICATION UNDER ORDER XXXIX RULES 1 AND 2 OF THE CODE OF CIVIL PROCEDURE, 1908
FOR GRANT OF AD-INTERIM/TEMPORARY INJUNCTION

MOST RESPECTFULLY SHOWETH:

1. That the plaintiff has filed the above-captioned suit against the defendant for [brief description of relief sought in the main suit — e.g., permanent injunction / specific performance / recovery of money / declaration of title].

2. That the present application is filed under Order XXXIX Rules 1 and 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, for grant of a temporary injunction restraining the defendant from [describe the act to be restrained — e.g., alienating/encumbering/transferring the suit property / interfering with plaintiff's possession / continuing the infringing acts / publishing/disseminating the information in question], pending the hearing and final disposal of the suit.

3. **Prima Facie Case:**
   That the plaintiff has a strong prima facie case. [Set out the basis of the plaintiff's claim in 2-3 paragraphs: the right, title, or interest asserted; the underlying agreement, deed, or entitlement; and how the defendant's conduct threatens or violates that right. Cite the key document or fact that makes the case prima facie believable.]

4. **Balance of Convenience:**
   That the balance of convenience lies overwhelmingly in favour of the plaintiff and against the defendant. If the temporary injunction is not granted:
   (a) [State the specific harm that will occur to the plaintiff if relief is denied — loss of property, business disruption, loss of goodwill, etc.]
   (b) On the other hand, if the injunction is granted and the plaintiff ultimately fails, the defendant would suffer only [minimal/no] prejudice, as [state why — e.g., the defendant has alternative resources / the act can be resumed if injunction is vacated / no significant expense has been incurred].

5. **Irreparable Injury:**
   That the plaintiff would suffer irreparable injury and loss if the temporary injunction is not granted, in that:
   (a) [Specific irreparable harm — e.g., the suit property is unique and no money compensation can restore the plaintiff's position; OR the defendant is dissipating assets making any eventual decree unenforceable; OR the continuous infringement is destroying the plaintiff's market share and goodwill which cannot be quantified in money.]
   (b) Monetary compensation, if granted at the stage of final decree, would not adequately redress the plaintiff's injury.

6. That the plaintiff has not suppressed any material facts from this Hon'ble Court and has approached this Court with clean hands.

7. That no similar application has been made before this Court or any other court, save and except [state any prior application if made].

8. That it is necessary in the interest of justice that an ex-parte ad-interim injunction be granted pending service of notice on the defendant because [state urgency — imminent sale, immediate threat of physical interference, irreversible act about to be taken].

**PRAYER**

It is, therefore, most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:

(a) **Grant an ad-interim injunction** ex-parte restraining the defendant, their servants, agents, and representatives from [specific act to be restrained], pending service and hearing on the present application; and

(b) **Confirm the said injunction** and make it absolute pending the hearing and final disposal of [Suit/Petition No. ___]; and

(c) Pass such other and further order(s) as this Hon'ble Court may deem fit and proper.
