# Written Statement Skill

## Governing Law
CPC 1908, Order VIII — Written Statement, Set-off and Counter-claim
- O.VIII R.1: Must be filed within 30 days; court may extend to 90 days (no further extension after)
- O.VIII R.3: Each allegation in plaint must be specifically admitted or denied
- O.VIII R.5: Every allegation not specifically denied is deemed admitted
- O.VIII R.6: Counterclaim as if a plaint in a cross-suit
- O.VIII R.6A: Counterclaim may be filed any time before final judgment

## Party Labels
- Filer: **Defendant** (in a civil suit)
- Opposing: **Plaintiff**

## The Most Critical Rule in Written Statement Practice

**PRELIMINARY OBJECTIONS MUST COME FIRST — BEFORE ANY ADMISSION OR DENIAL ON MERITS.**

Why: If a jurisdiction objection exists (territorial, pecuniary, or subject matter), raising it after making admissions on merits waives the objection. The court will treat the defendant as having submitted to jurisdiction. This is the most common fatal error in written statements.

**If there is any jurisdictional argument at all — even a weak one — it must appear in Preliminary Objections Para 1, before the word "Without prejudice to the above" that opens the para-by-para reply.**

## Required Sections (in this exact order)

```
1. court_header          → Formal court name
2. suit_details          → Suit No., year, parties ("In the matter of [Plaintiff] v. [Defendant]")
3. opening               → "WRITTEN STATEMENT ON BEHALF OF DEFENDANT NO. __"
4. preliminary_objections → ALL threshold objections — limitation, jurisdiction, non-maintainability
5. para_admissions_denials → Para-by-para response tracking plaintiff's numbering
6. affirmative_defences  → Defendant's positive case (setoff, payment, estoppel, waiver, etc.)
7. counterclaim          → [CONDITIONAL: if defendant has an affirmative claim against plaintiff]
8. prayer                → Dismissal of suit + costs; counterclaim relief if any
9. verification          → Defendant's verification
```

## Section-by-Section Drafting Instructions

### preliminary_objections
```
PRELIMINARY OBJECTIONS

1. [Jurisdiction objection — always first if it exists]:
   That the present suit is not maintainable before this Hon'ble Court as this
   Court has no territorial jurisdiction to try the same. [The Plaintiff does not
   reside within / the cause of action did not arise within / the defendant does not
   carry on business within] the territorial jurisdiction of this Court.

2. [Limitation objection]:
   That the suit is barred by the law of limitation. The cause of action, if any,
   arose on [date] and the suit has been filed on [filing date], which is beyond
   the period of [X years] prescribed under Article [X] of Schedule I to the
   Limitation Act, 1963.

3. [Non-maintainability / Cause of action]:
   That the plaint does not disclose any cause of action against the defendant.
   [The plaintiff has suppressed material facts / The plaintiff has no locus standi
   to file the present suit].

4. [Any other threshold objection]:
   That the suit is barred by [res judicata / constructive res judicata / Order II
   Rule 2 CPC / the doctrine of election / an arbitration clause].
```

**Always include limitation objection** unless you are certain the suit is within time. Even if the suit is in time, state "Without prejudice" if raising it protects the client.

### para_admissions_denials

**The most important structural rule:** Para-by-para response must track the PLAINT'S numbering. If the plaint has Para 4, the written statement must have a response to Para 4. Never skip, renumber, or regroup plaint paragraphs.

```
REPLY ON MERITS
(Without prejudice to the preliminary objections above)

Para 1: The contents of Para 1 of the plaint are [admitted / denied / admitted
         in part and denied in part]. [Explanation if partial.]

Para 2: The contents of Para 2 of the plaint are denied as being false, wrong,
         and not admitted. [State defendant's version of the same facts.]

Para 3: The contents of Para 3 of the plaint are a matter of record and need
         no admission or denial. [Use for averments about documents filed in court.]

Para 4: The contents of Para 4 of the plaint are denied. It is specifically
         denied that [repeat the specific allegation being denied, then state
         what actually happened from defendant's perspective].
```

**Rules for admissions/denials:**
- "Denied" alone is insufficient — must state defendant's version of the fact
- "Not admitted" = the defendant has no knowledge and requires proof
- "Admitted in part" — specify exactly which part
- Do not use evasive denials ("denied as stated" / "not within knowledge of the defendant" for facts within their knowledge) — courts ignore these
- O.VIII R.5: Anything not denied is deemed admitted

### affirmative_defences
Include any positive defences the defendant has:

```
AFFIRMATIVE DEFENCE

1. Set-off: That the plaintiff is indebted to the defendant in a sum of
   Rs. [X] on account of [specify the cross-claim]. The defendant is entitled
   to set off this sum against any amount decreed in favour of the plaintiff.

2. Payment: That the defendant has already paid Rs. [X] on [dates] as evidenced
   by receipts [Annexure D-1 to D-3]. The suit for recovery of this amount is
   therefore not maintainable.

3. Accord and satisfaction: That the parties entered into a full and final
   settlement on [date] and the plaintiff accepted [amount] in full satisfaction
   of all claims.

4. Estoppel / Waiver: That the plaintiff is estopped from making the present
   claim because [the plaintiff's own conduct / representation / acquiescence
   induced the defendant to believe that the claim had been waived].
```

### counterclaim (conditional — O.VIII R.6A)
```
COUNTER-CLAIM

[Heading as per Rule 6A: "COUNTER-CLAIM UNDER ORDER VIII RULE 6A CPC"]

The defendant states and claims as follows:

1. That [facts giving rise to defendant's cross-claim against plaintiff].

2. That the plaintiff is indebted to the defendant in the sum of Rs. [X]
   by reason of [cause of counterclaim].

[Prayer in counterclaim:]
The defendant prays that the plaintiff's suit be dismissed and the defendant
be awarded Rs. [X] together with interest at [X]% per annum from [date] till
realisation, with costs.
```

**When to use:** When defendant has an affirmative claim against plaintiff that arises from the same transaction or relates to the same subject matter.

### prayer
```
PRAYER

In view of the foregoing, it is humbly submitted that this Hon'ble Court
may be pleased to:

(a) Dismiss the present suit with costs;

(b) [If counterclaim filed]:
    Decree the counterclaim in favour of the defendant for Rs. [X] together
    with interest at [X]% per annum;

(c) Pass such other and further orders as this Hon'ble Court may deem fit
    in the interest of justice.
```

### verification
```
VERIFICATION

I, [Defendant's name], [designation/occupation], the defendant herein,
do hereby verify that the contents of paragraphs [1 to __] of this written
statement are true to my knowledge and the contents of paragraphs [__ to __]
are believed by me to be true on the basis of information received and
believed to be true.

Verified at [Place] on [Date].

[Signature]
Defendant No. ___
```

**Note:** Split verification by personal knowledge vs. information — a careless blanket "all paragraphs are true to my knowledge" creates perjury risk for paragraphs the defendant knows only from documents.

## Common Errors to Avoid

1. **Preliminary objections AFTER merits**: Fatal — jurisdiction objection is waived. Always raise threshold objections before "without prejudice" reply on merits.
2. **Not tracking plaint paragraph numbers**: If plaint has Para 8, the WS must respond to Para 8 by that number. Regrouping = deemed admission of skipped paragraphs.
3. **"Denied as stated" without defendant's version**: Courts treat this as an evasive denial. Always state what actually happened from defendant's perspective.
4. **Blanket denials**: "All allegations are denied" — invalid. O.VIII R.3 requires specific denial of each specific allegation.
5. **Missing limitation objection in preliminary objections**: Even if limitation is arguable, raise it in preliminary objections or it may be deemed waived.
6. **Counterclaim after trial begins**: Counterclaim under O.VIII R.6A must be filed before final hearing; after decree it becomes a separate suit.
7. **Broad verification**: Split verification into personal knowledge vs. information received — do not verify external facts as personally known.
