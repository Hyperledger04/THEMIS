# Legal Notice Skill

## When to Use This Skill
Use when the matter requires serving a formal notice on a party before initiating legal proceedings, or when a party needs to assert a legal right and demand action. Legal notices are often a statutory prerequisite to filing suit (e.g., S.80 CPC against government bodies) or a commercial necessity before approaching a court.

## Document Types Covered
- Demand notices (money recovery, cheque dishonour, property disputes)
- Pre-suit statutory notices under S.80 CPC (mandatory before suing government/public officers)
- Notices under S.138 Negotiable Instruments Act (cheque dishonour)
- Notices to vacate (landlord-tenant)
- Notices for breach of contract
- Consumer notices under Consumer Protection Act, 2019

## Mandatory Intake Checklist
Before drafting, confirm all of the following:
- [ ] Full legal name and address of sender (your client)
- [ ] Full legal name and address of recipient (the noticee)
- [ ] Specific demand — amount, action, or relief sought
- [ ] Time period given for compliance (15 days / 30 days / 60 days — check the applicable statute)
- [ ] Whether this is a statutory notice (e.g., S.80 CPC — 2-month period mandatory)
- [ ] Date of cause of action (e.g., date of dishonour, date of breach, date of default)
- [ ] Mode of service (registered post AD / email / personal service — choose at least two)

## Structure Template

```
[LETTERHEAD / ADVOCATE'S NAME AND ENROLLMENT NO.]
[Date]

LEGAL NOTICE

From:
[Full name and address of sender / client]
Through their Advocate: [Advocate's name, enrollment, address]

To:
[Full name and address of recipient — each noticee on a separate "To:" line]

Subject: Legal Notice for [brief subject — e.g., Recovery of Rs. ___/- / Vacation of Premises]

Sir / Madam,

Under the instructions and on behalf of my client, [CLIENT NAME], I hereby serve you with this
Legal Notice:

1. [Identity of my client and the relationship between parties]
2. [Background facts — numbered paragraphs]
3. [The specific default, breach, or wrong committed]
4. [Applicable law / contractual provision violated]
5. [The specific demand / relief sought]
6. [Time period for compliance — e.g., "within 15 (fifteen) days of receipt of this notice"]
7. [Consequences of non-compliance — legal proceedings, costs, damages]

You are hereby called upon to [STATE THE SPECIFIC DEMAND] within [TIME PERIOD] from the
date of receipt of this notice, failing which my client shall be constrained to initiate
appropriate legal proceedings against you before the competent court/forum, entirely at
your risk, cost, and consequences.

This notice is being served upon you by Registered Post AD and Speed Post.

[Advocate's Signature]
[Advocate's Name]
[Enrollment No.]
[Address]
[Date]

Note: A copy of this notice is retained in my client's file.
```

## Risk Flags
- **HIGH:** S.80 CPC notice — mandatory 2-month waiting period before suing government or public officer. Filing before the period expires is a fatal defect (suit is premature).
- **HIGH:** S.138 NI Act notice — must be sent within 30 days of receiving the bank's memo of return/dishonour. Missing this window extinguishes the criminal remedy.
- **HIGH:** Address accuracy — notice sent to wrong address may not constitute valid service. Verify the registered/last known address.
- **MEDIUM:** Time period stated in the notice — must match the statutory minimum (do not give less than the statute requires).
- **MEDIUM:** Serve by at least two modes (e.g., registered post + email + WhatsApp with delivery receipt) so proof of service is easy to establish.
- **LOW:** Keep a copy of the notice and postal receipt — these are evidence in the subsequent suit.

## Key Statutes
- Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 — S.80 (government notices), O.XXXVII (summary suits)
- Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 — S.138 (cheque dishonour, 15-day notice after bank memo)
- Transfer of Property Act, 1882 — S.106 (notice to quit for tenancy)
- Consumer Protection Act, 2019 — S.35 (pre-filing notice to opposite party)
- Specific Relief Act, 1963 — S.21 (notice before suit for specific performance)

## Output Rules
- Always state the demand with precision: exact rupee amount, exact property address, exact action required
- Always state the deadline with the number in both digits and words: "15 (fifteen) days"
- State the consequences of non-compliance explicitly
- Include the advocate's enrollment number and address (required for a valid legal notice)
- Mention the mode of service at the end of the notice
- Do not include casual language — tone is formal and unambiguous throughout
