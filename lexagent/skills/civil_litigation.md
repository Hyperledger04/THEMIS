# Civil Litigation Skill

## When to Use This Skill
Use when the matter involves any civil court proceeding under the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC). This includes suits, applications, and appeals in civil courts at any level.

## Document Types Covered
- Plaints (O.VII CPC)
- Written Statements (O.VIII CPC)
- Interim Injunction Applications (O.XXXIX R.1&2 CPC)
- Permanent Injunction Suits (S.38 Specific Relief Act)
- Execution Petitions (O.XXI CPC)
- First Appeals / Second Appeals
- Applications under S.151 CPC (inherent powers)
- Interlocutory Applications (IAs)

## Mandatory Intake Checklist
Before drafting, verify all of the following are confirmed:
- [ ] Cause title and exact name of the court
- [ ] Limitation period checked (identify applicable Article in Schedule I, Limitation Act 1963)
- [ ] Jurisdiction — territorial (where defendant resides or cause of action arose), pecuniary (court fee valuation), and subject matter
- [ ] Relief sought — stated specifically and quantified where applicable
- [ ] Valuation for court fee computation
- [ ] All necessary parties impleaded (O.I R.10 CPC)

## Structure Template

### For Injunction Applications (O.XXXIX R.1&2 CPC)
```
IN THE [COURT NAME]
[CASE TYPE] NO. ___ OF ____

[PLAINTIFF NAME]                         ... Plaintiff/Applicant
                   versus
[DEFENDANT NAME]                         ... Defendant/Respondent

APPLICATION UNDER ORDER XXXIX RULES 1 AND 2 READ WITH SECTION 151 CPC

Most Respectfully Showeth:

1. [Brief facts — numbered paragraphs]
2. [Nature of threat / imminent injury]
3. [Three-pronged test — prima facie case, balance of convenience, irreparable harm]

CAUSE OF ACTION
JURISDICTION
LIMITATION
RELIEF SOUGHT / PRAYER
VERIFICATION
```

### For Plaints (O.VII CPC)
```
IN THE [COURT NAME]
SUIT NO. ___ OF ____

[PLAINTIFF NAME]                         ... Plaintiff
                   versus
[DEFENDANT NAME]                         ... Defendant

PLAINT

Most Respectfully Showeth:

1. [Parties]
2. [Facts giving rise to the suit — numbered paragraphs]
3. [Cause of action]
4. [Jurisdiction]
5. [Limitation]
6. [Valuation and Court Fees]
7. [Relief sought]

PRAYER
VERIFICATION
```

## Risk Flags
- **HIGH:** Any ground for limitation bar — check the applicable Article in Schedule I and plead specifically. If time-barred, the suit will be dismissed at threshold.
- **HIGH:** Jurisdiction challenge — always plead all three heads (territorial, pecuniary, subject matter). Missing even one opens a preliminary objection.
- **HIGH:** For injunctions — all three elements must be specifically pleaded: (1) prima facie case, (2) balance of convenience, (3) irreparable harm/injury.
- **MEDIUM:** Impleadment of necessary parties (O.I R.10) — missing parties can cause a decree to be unenforceable.
- **MEDIUM:** Valuation errors in court fees — can result in objection and delay.
- **LOW:** Verification clause — must be in first person, signed by the deponent, with place and date.

## Key Statutes
- Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (especially Orders VII, VIII, XXXIX, XXI)
- Limitation Act, 1963 (Schedule I for limitation periods)
- Specific Relief Act, 1963 (S.38 permanent injunction; S.14 for specific performance)
- Registration Act, 1908 (property matters — unregistered documents inadmissible)
- Transfer of Property Act, 1882 (property disputes)

## Mandatory Citation Patterns

### For Interim Injunctions
Always cite the three-test standard:
- *Dalpat Kumar v. Prahlad Singh* (1991) 4 SCC 130 — the canonical statement of the three-pronged test
- *Gujarat Bottling Co. Ltd. v. Coca Cola Co.* (1995) 5 SCC 545 — irreparable harm and balance of convenience
- *Morgan Stanley Mutual Fund v. Kartick Das* (1994) 4 SCC 225 — when to grant/refuse ex parte injunction

### For Specific Performance
- *Saradamani Kandappan v. S. Rajalakshmi* (2011) 12 SCC 18 — readiness and willingness

## Output Rules
- Number every paragraph of facts (1, 2, 3…)
- **Bold the prayer clause header**
- Include a verification clause at the end of every document
- Separate sheet for court fee computation if the relief has a pecuniary value
- Date and place of signing must appear in the verification
- Use "Most Respectfully Showeth:" for applications, "Respectfully Submitted:" for written arguments
