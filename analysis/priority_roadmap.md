## P0 — Filing-Blocking (Do these or the output cannot be filed)

### 1. Strip lawyer working notes from .docx

**The problem:** `base_system.md:42–46` instructs the LLM to append Plain English Summary + Risk Assessment to `draft_output` after a `---` separator. `docx_writer.py:65–79` writes the entire string to the .docx without parsing this separator. Every court filing currently contains the lawyer's internal risk flags and client summary.

**The fix:**
- `lexagent/tools/docx_writer.py:65–79` — split `draft_output` at the first `---` separator. Write only the content before it to the filing .docx. Write everything after it to a separate `lawyer_notes.docx`.
- `lexagent/tools/docx_writer.py:99–107` — remove the "Generated by LexAgent | Phase 5 draft" footer from the filing .docx. Move it to `lawyer_notes.docx`.

**Effort:** 1 hour. Zero architectural change. Pure string parsing.

---

### 2. Fix party labels by matter type

**The problem:** `docx_writer.py:49–55` guesses party labels from dict keys: `parties.get("plaintiff") or parties.get("petitioner")`. For an S.138 criminal complaint, this produces "Petitioner v. Respondent" — writ petition labels. The correct labels are "Complainant ... Accused."

**The fix:**
- Add a `PARTY_LABELS` map in `docx_writer.py`:
  ```python
  PARTY_LABELS = {
      "s138_complaint": ("Complainant", "Accused"),
      "bail_application": ("Accused", None),
      "writ_petition": ("Petitioner", "Respondent"),
      "plaint": ("Plaintiff", "Defendant"),
      "injunction_application": ("Plaintiff/Applicant", "Defendant/Respondent"),
  }
  ```
- Read `state["matter_type"]` to select the correct label pair.
- Default to ("Petitioner", "Respondent") if matter_type not in map.

**Effort:** 30 minutes.

### 3. Implement formal court header from SOUL.md

**The problem:** `docx_writer.py:43–47` generates `matter_type.title()` as Heading 1 ("Legal Document", "Complaint"). A court filing's first line is the court's full formal name: "IN THE COURT OF THE CHIEF JUDICIAL MAGISTRATE, GAUTAM BUDH NAGAR AT GREATER NOIDA."

**The fix — two steps:**

Step A (immediate, no setup wizard): Parse `state["jurisdiction"]` and format it as a court header. Use all-caps, centered, no heading style — a plain paragraph with bold formatting.

Step B (complete): Extend `lex setup` to ask the lawyer for their court name and formatting preferences. Store in `~/.lexagent/SOUL.md` under `## Court Preferences`. Load in `docx_writer.py` via `load_soul()` (already exists in `nodes/draft.py`).

Fields to add to `lex setup` (see `formatting_engine.md` for full question list):
- `court_name_formal` — full formal name for the header
- `font_name`, `font_size`, `line_spacing`, `margin_left_inches`
- `exhibit_label_format` — EX-CW1/A vs Annexure P-1 vs custom
- `cause_title_example` — lawyer pastes their own cause title format

**Files:** `lexagent/tools/docx_writer.py:43–60`, `lexagent/cli.py` (setup wizard), `lexagent/nodes/intake.py` (setup wizard alternative entry point).

**Effort:** Step A = 1 hour. Step B (full setup wizard extension) = 1 day.

### 4. Add dedicated S.138 Complaint skill

**The problem:** `templates/legal_notice_s138.md` is a demand notice template. When a lawyer requests an S.138 complaint, the retrieve node injects a demand notice as the structural reference. The two documents are structurally different: different party labels, different section structure, no affidavit in a notice, different prayer.

**The fix:** Create `lexagent/skills/s138_complaint.md` with:
- Frontmatter: `name: s138_complaint`, `trigger_keywords: [138 complaint, s138 complaint, cheque dishonour complaint, ni act complaint]`, `matter_types: [s138_complaint]`
- Full 14-section structure (from `pleading_blueprints.md`)
- S.141 conditional block: "If the accused is a proprietor, partner, or director of a firm/company, add a Section 141 paragraph after the accused identity paragraph"
- Prayer wording: explicitly "fine equivalent to twice the amount of the cheque i.e. Rs. [cheque_amount × 2]"
- Exhibit labels: EX-CW1/A format
- Sub-documents required: affidavit-in-evidence, witness list, list of documents
- *Laxmi Dyechem* v. *State of Gujarat* [2012] 11 SCR 466 — cite for signature mismatch dishonour

**Files:** New `lexagent/skills/s138_complaint.md`

**Effort:** 2–3 hours (writing the skill content).

## P1 — Structurally Weak (Fileable but will draw objections)

### 5. Generate affidavit-in-evidence as a separate sub-document

**The problem:** The current output is one .docx with a verification clause at the end. A complaint requires a standalone affidavit-in-evidence — the complainant's sworn testimony that is the examination-in-chief in a summary trial under S.143 NI Act.

**The fix:**
- In `nodes/draft.py`, after the main draft is generated, make a second LLM call:
  ```
  "Based on the complaint facts, draft an Evidence by Affidavit for the complainant.
   Write 12–14 numbered paragraphs in first person. The deponent is {complainant_name}.
   Reference each exhibit by its label from the exhibit registry: {exhibit_registry}.
   End with a verification block."
  ```
- Write the affidavit to `affidavit_evidence.docx` via a new `write_affidavit_docx()` function in `docx_writer.py`.
- Return `state["affidavit_output"]` and `state["affidavit_path"]` from the draft node.

**Files:** `lexagent/nodes/draft.py`, `lexagent/tools/docx_writer.py` (new function), `lexagent/state.py` (new fields).

**Effort:** 1 day.

### 6. Build exhibit registry before drafting

**The problem:** Exhibit labels (EX-CW1/A, EX-CW1/B…) are chosen inline by the LLM. If the LLM generates the complaint body and the affidavit independently, the labels will drift. "The invoice (EX-CW1/A)" in the complaint and "the invoice (Annexure A)" in the affidavit refer to the same document — the court officer must reconcile this discrepancy.

**The fix:**
- In `nodes/intake.py` (or a new pre-draft step), build an exhibit registry from the extracted facts:
  ```python
  exhibit_registry = build_exhibit_registry(matter_facts, court_profile)
  # Returns: {"invoice": "EX-CW1/A", "cheque": "EX-CW1/B", "return_memo_1": "EX-CW1/C", ...}
  ```
- Store in `LexState["exhibit_registry"]`.
- Inject into every draft prompt: "Use only these exhibit labels. Do not invent new labels."
- Use the same registry in the affidavit draft, the list of documents, and the witness list.

The exhibit label format comes from SOUL.md court preferences (EX-CW1/A vs Annexure P-1 etc.).

**Files:** `lexagent/nodes/intake.py`, `lexagent/nodes/draft.py`, `lexagent/state.py` (new `exhibit_registry` field).

**Effort:** Half a day.

### 7. Structural section check in review node

**The problem:** `nodes/review.py:44–79` only checks word count and citation verification. A complaint missing its CAUSE OF ACTION, JURISDICTION, or LIMITATION paragraphs passes review without warning.

**The fix:**
- Add a `REQUIRED_SECTION_KEYWORDS` dict:
  ```python
  REQUIRED_SECTION_KEYWORDS = {
      "s138_complaint": ["cause of action", "jurisdiction", "limitation", "prayer"],
      "writ_petition": ["grounds", "prayer", "verification"],
      "plaint": ["cause of action", "jurisdiction", "limitation", "prayer", "verification"],
      "bail_application": ["prayer"],
  }
  ```
- After word count check, scan `draft_output.lower()` for each keyword.
- If any required keyword is missing, add a `StructuralWarning` to `risk_annotations` with severity `P0`.
- P0 structural warnings should block the output (return error for re-draft), not just annotate.

**Files:** `lexagent/nodes/review.py:44–79`.

**Effort:** 2 hours.

### 8. S.141 conditional block in draft instruction

**The problem:** Section 141 NI Act extends liability to proprietors, partners, and directors of a firm/company when the firm is the accused. The AI draft misses this because `accused_entity_type` is not an intake field, and no conditional block fires S.141.

**The fix — two steps:**

Step A: Add `accused_entity_type` to the intake question bank (`lexagent/data/intake_questions.yaml`):
```yaml
- id: accused_entity_type
  question: "Is the accused an individual, a proprietorship firm, a partnership firm, or a company?"
  matter_types: [s138_complaint]
  options: [individual, proprietorship, partnership, company]
```

Step B: In `nodes/draft.py:192–281`, add a conditional block to the draft instruction:
```python
if state.get("accused_entity_type") in ("proprietorship", "partnership", "company"):
    draft_instruction += "\n\nIMPORTANT: The accused is a {accused_entity_type}. "
    draft_instruction += "Include a paragraph invoking Section 141 NI Act after the accused identity paragraph. "
    draft_instruction += "State that {accused_name} was personally responsible for the day-to-day conduct of the business."
```

**Files:** `lexagent/data/intake_questions.yaml`, `lexagent/nodes/draft.py:192–281`, `lexagent/state.py` (add `accused_entity_type` field).

**Effort:** 2–3 hours.

## P2 — Quality Improvements

### 9. Limitation and jurisdiction as computed fields

**The problem:** The LLM generates the limitation and jurisdiction paragraphs from prose. These should be arithmetic, not language modelling:
- Limitation (S.142(b) NI Act): `notice_date + 15 days (compliance period) + 30 days (statutory window)` = last date to file complaint
- Jurisdiction (S.142(2) NI Act): court with territorial jurisdiction over the branch of the bank where the cheque was presented for collection

**The fix:**
- In `nodes/intake.py`, after extracting `notice_date` and `presenting_bank_branch`:
  ```python
  notice_compliance_deadline = notice_date + timedelta(days=15)
  limitation_deadline = notice_compliance_deadline + timedelta(days=30)
  jurisdiction_court = resolve_court_from_bank_branch(presenting_bank_branch)
  ```
- Store as `LexState["limitation_deadline"]`, `LexState["jurisdiction_court"]`
- Inject into draft instruction as verified facts: "The limitation deadline is {limitation_deadline}. The court with jurisdiction is {jurisdiction_court} per S.142(2)."
- The LLM uses these values verbatim — it does not compute them.

**Files:** `lexagent/nodes/intake.py`, `lexagent/state.py`.

**Effort:** Half a day.

### 10. `lex add-reference` command for structure RAG

**The problem:** Draft structure is learned from developer-written templates that may not match the lawyer's actual court conventions. The lawyer's own past filings are a better structural reference.

**The fix:** Add a CLI command:
```bash
lex add-reference COMPLAINT-138.docx --type s138_complaint --court gbncjm
```

This command:
1. Reads the .docx and extracts text (via `python-docx`)
2. Chunks the text by paragraph/section
3. Embeds chunks and stores in Qdrant collection `reference_documents` with metadata: `{doc_type, court, lawyer_id, year}`
4. At draft time, `nodes/retrieve.py` queries this collection first (before the SQLite past-drafts query): `{"doc_type": "s138_complaint", "court": "gbncjm"}` → returns top 2 matching chunks
5. Retrieved reference is injected into the LLM draft instruction: "Here is an example of how you have drafted this document type before. Mirror the section structure, cause title style, prayer wording, and exhibit labels."

**Skills integration:** Skills can declare `reference_collection: s138_complaints` in their YAML frontmatter. The skill loader auto-fetches matching reference documents when loading the skill.

**Files:** `lexagent/cli.py` (new command), `lexagent/nodes/retrieve.py` (add Qdrant reference query), `lexagent/skills/loader.py` (add `reference_collection` frontmatter support).

**Effort:** 1–2 days.

## Final Question

> "If this system produced perfect drafts, what would still stop lawyers from filing directly?"

1. **Wet signature.** Every pleading requires the advocate's and client's physical signature. The .docx must be printed and signed. Automation cannot sign.

2. **Court fee stamp.** Most Indian courts require a physical court fee stamp (judicial stamp paper or e-stamp) affixed to the original. The system can compute the fee amount accurately; it cannot affix the stamp.

3. **Filing counter discretion.** Registry clerks apply local conventions — specific paper size (F/C size vs A4), binding style, number of hole punches, order of documents in the bundle. These vary clerk-to-clerk and are largely undocumented. A filing can be returned for "format defects" that no system can predict.

4. **Advocate's enrollment number.** The vakalatnama requires the advocate's Bar Council enrollment number. If SOUL.md does not contain it, the vakalatnama is incomplete. This is a one-time setup item, not a structural problem.

5. **Oath commissioner for affidavit.** The complainant must personally depose to the affidavit before an oath commissioner (a gazetted officer or notary). The affidavit cannot be filed unfiled — it requires a physical appearance and the oath commissioner's stamp and signature.

6. **Same-day service requirement.** In some matters, copies must be served on opposite parties on the date of filing or before the first hearing. The system can prepare the service copies; the physical act of service requires a human.

The system can eliminate all the intellectual labour from drafting. The physical acts — signing, stamping, deposing, serving — will always require the lawyer.
