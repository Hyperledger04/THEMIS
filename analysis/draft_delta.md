## Paragraph-Level Comparison

### Opening / Court Header

**AI Draft:**
```
Legal Document
Petitioner v. Respondent
[jurisdiction line, italic]
```

**Human Filing:**
```
IN THE COURT OF THE CHIEF JUDICIAL MAGISTRATE,
GAUTAM BUDH NAGAR AT GREATER NOIDA

COMPLAINT CASE NO. _____ OF 2026

M/s. Pinnacle Systems,
a proprietary concern owned and operated by
Sh. Ankush Sareen ...                          COMPLAINANT

                  VERSUS

1. Mr. Santosh                                  ACCUSED
2. Mrs. Shilpy Yadav                            ACCUSED

COMPLAINT UNDER SECTION 138 READ WITH SECTION 142 OF THE
NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENTS ACT, 1881
```

**What changed:** The human draft establishes jurisdiction (which court), matter type (which statute), and party roles (Complainant/Accused) in the first 10 lines. A court officer reading page one knows immediately: district criminal court, NI Act complaint, two accused. The AI draft says "Legal Document / Petitioner v. Respondent" — which gives no information useful to a court officer.

### Section 141 — Corporate/Firm Liability

**AI Draft:** Not present.

**Human Filing (Para 10):**
> _"That the Accused No. 1, Mr. Santosh, was at all material times the sole proprietor of M/s. Rudransh Cosmetics / Nice Collection, and was personally responsible for and in charge of the day-to-day conduct of the business of the said firm. The Accused No. 2, Mrs. Shilpy Yadav, was the drawer of the cheque and was equally responsible for the said dishonoured cheque. The accused persons are therefore jointly and severally liable under Section 138 read with Section 141 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881."_

**What changed:** S.141 extends criminal liability to persons in charge of a firm/company. Without this paragraph, the accused can argue that only the signatory (Mrs. Shilpy Yadav) is liable, not the proprietor. With it, both accused are pinned. The AI draft missed this because `accused_entity_type` is not an intake field, and the skill has no conditional S.141 block.

### Prayer

**AI Draft (Reliefs A–E):**
```
(A) Take cognizance of the offence under Section 138 of the NI Act
(B) Issue process/summons against the accused
(C) Convict the accused and award appropriate sentence
(D) Direct payment of the cheque amount as compensation
(E) Award costs of litigation
```

**Human Filing (Reliefs 1–4):**
```
(a) Take cognizance of the complaint and issue process/summons
(b) Upon conviction, award sentence of imprisonment and fine equivalent to
    twice the amount of the cheque i.e. Rs. 2,14,810/- (Rupees Two Lakh
    Fourteen Thousand Eight Hundred and Ten Only)
(c) Direct payment of the said amount as compensation under S.357 CrPC
(d) Award costs of proceedings
```

**What changed:** Prayer (b) in the human draft specifies "fine equivalent to twice the cheque amount" — this is the maximum punishment under S.138 (imprisonment up to 2 years or fine up to twice the cheque amount or both). Specifying it explicitly creates the record for the court to award the maximum. "Appropriate sentence" in the AI draft gives the court discretion to award less. This is a matter of sentencing strategy, not legal error — but it is the kind of thing a senior practitioner always includes.

---

### Affidavit-in-Evidence

**AI Draft:** No standalone affidavit. A verification clause at paragraph 26:
> _"I, Ankush Sareen, do hereby verify that the contents of this complaint are true and correct to the best of my knowledge..."_

**Human Filing:** Separate document — "EVIDENCE BY AFFIDAVIT OF SH. ANKUSH SAREEN (CW-1)" — 13 numbered paragraphs restating every fact from the complaint in first-person sworn testimony, plus:
- Explicit reference to each exhibit (EX-CW1/A etc.)
- Statement that all documents are true copies
- Oath commissioner signature block

**What changed:** In a summary trial under Section 143 NI Act, evidence is often taken by affidavit instead of oral examination-in-chief. The affidavit IS the evidence. Without it, the complainant has a pleading but no evidence document. The AI draft conflates the verification clause (which attests that the pleading is true) with the evidence affidavit (which is the sworn testimony on which the court decides the case). These are two different documents serving two different legal functions.

## Invisible Lawyer Work: What Happened Between Draft A and Final Filing

The human draft did not change the facts. It reorganized them. Here is the complete list of decisions the lawyer made between receiving the AI draft and filing:

1. **Reframed from a letter to a court document.** Stripped the letterhead format. Added the court header block with formal court designation, blank case number, and cause title using criminal complaint party labels (Complainant/Accused, not Petitioner/Respondent).

2. **Separated the document packet.** Broke the monolithic AI draft into: Complaint body → List of Documents → Evidence Affidavit → Witness List. Each is a distinct legal function.

3. **Stripped all lawyer working notes from the filing.** The Plain English Summary, Risk Assessment (with HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW flags), and Matter ID footer are internal tools — they belong in a separate lawyer memo, not in the court record.

4. **Added the S.141 paragraph.** The accused are a proprietor + wife (drawer). S.141 pins both under the same criminal liability. The AI draft missed this because it does not have a conditional block keyed on accused entity type.

5. **Grounded the Laxmi Dyechem citation** with the correct neutral citation [2012] 11 SCR 466 and reproduced the specific holding on signature mismatch. The AI draft noted "Citations Requiring Verification" — meaning the citation was flagged but not resolved.

6. **Changed exhibit labels** from "Annexure A" to "EX-CW1/A". The format EX-CW{n}/{alpha} is the district court standard: CW = court witness, number = witness sequence, alpha = exhibit sequence for that witness. This matters because during trial, the court officer uses these labels to locate physical documents.

7. **Drafted the standalone affidavit.** 13 paragraphs, first person, by complainant, with exhibit cross-references. This is the evidence document, not the pleading.

8. **Added the witness list.** CW-1 is the complainant (proves all facts). Bank officials are listed to prove the return memo without requiring them to appear (they can be subpoenaed if contested). The AI draft has no concept of witnesses.

9. **Corrected the prayer.** Changed "appropriate fine" to "fine equivalent to twice the cheque amount" = ₹2,14,810 (explicitly computed). This is the standard S.138 prayer used by experienced practitioners.

10. **Added explicit limitation and jurisdiction paragraphs** anchored to specific statutory sub-sections: S.142(b) for limitation (30 days from expiry of notice period), S.142(2) for jurisdiction (court where cheque was delivered for collection = GBN, where SBI Bisrakh Jalalpur is located). The AI draft mentioned both but did not cite the sub-sections.

11. **Removed the Vakalatnama from the complaint body.** It is filed separately at the court counter. Embedding it in the complaint body is a format error.
